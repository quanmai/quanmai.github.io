Abstract Thinking

When listenning to popcasts, I realize that many great researchers have an ability to explain complex ideas in an abstract yet intuitive way. This does not mean they cannot explain things in technical details; rather, they can distill complex concepts into simpler, more relatable explainations. Abstract thinking requires a deep understanding of the subject and the ability to simplify things. Simplify difficult concepts is itself difficult. The deeper your understanding, the easier it becomes to break things down and communicate them clearly. Yet, I have found it challenging to do this myself. Therefore, I write this page to practice my abstract thinking, hopefully I can learn things deeply and explain things clearly.
