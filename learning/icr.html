Paper Notes - ICR

Aug 2, 2025

My notes on the paper Attention In LLMs Yields Efficient Zero-Shot Rerankers by Chen et al. (2024).

Why it's cool? - TL;DR

  • Innovation: Uses attention shifts, not generation, for re-ranking.
  • Efficiency: Just two forward passes ‚Üí O(1) time per query.
  • Calibration: Removes bias with a content‚Äëfree query baseline
  • Accessibility: Works on open models, no extra training.
  • Performance: Wins over RankGPT and scales faster.

Can attention reveal relevance?

Imagine you're organizing a talent show with thousands of performers (documents), but you only want the top 10 to go on stage. Current methods ask each performer to sing (generate text) before judging‚Äîcostly and slow. What if we could judge them just by watching where the model pays attention?

In-Context Re-Ranking

The authors introduce ICR, a method that leverages LLM attention without generation:

  • Format the query and candidate documents into a single prompt.
  • Extract the attention weights from query tokens to each document.
  • Run a second pass with a content-free query (e.g., "N/A").
  • Subtract attention baselines to yield bias-free relevance scores.

Just two forward passes, no training, no gradients‚Äîzero-shot and constant-time.

Findings

  • On MS MARCO, ICR outperforms RankGPT and ColBERTv2 by up to +8.5 MRR@10.
  • Improves multi-hop retrieval by +2.2 points.
  • Reduces inference time by over 60% compared to generative re-rankers.

Takeaway

This paper reimagines the LLM as a silent observer. Instead of making the model talk, we ask it to listen to where it pays attention. That attention becomes a powerful, scalable, and efficient signal for re-ranking documents.

Attention isn't just for learning‚Äîit's for retrieval.
